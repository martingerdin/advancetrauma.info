ADVANCE TRAUMA

Effects of Advanced Trauma Life Support® Training Compared to Standard Care on Adult Trauma Patient Outcomes: A Cluster Randomised Trial

About the Trial

Aim

To compare the effects of ATLS® training with standard care on trauma patient outcomes.

Primary Outcome

In-hospital mortality within 30 days of arrival at the emergency department.

Trial Design

A batched stepped-wedge cluster randomised trial conducted in India.

Trial Population

Adult trauma patients who present to the emergency department of participating hospitals and are admitted or transferred for admission.

Sample Size

30 clusters and 4320 patients.

Intervention

ATLS® training is a 2.5-day course teaching a standardized approach to trauma patient care, delivered at an accredited ATLS® training facility in India. The course focuses on primary and secondary survey concepts.

Hospitals: Secondary or tertiary hospitals in India that admit or refer/transfer for admission at least 400 trauma patients per year Clusters: One or more units of physicians providing initial trauma care in the emergency department of tertiary hospitals in India Patients: Adult trauma patients who present to the emergency department of participating hospitals and are admitted or transferred for admission
